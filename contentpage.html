Beyond the Screen

Video games have a dual and complex impact on youth mental health and social interactions, with both potential benefits and serious risks. The effects largely depend on the type of game, the amount of time spent playing, and the individual's pre-existing mental state.

The effects on mental health can be either positive or negative. On the positive edge, video games can be a source of stress relief and can help some teens cope with anxiety. They can improve cognitive functions like problem-solving, attention, and memory. The rewarding feeling of completing a level or winning a game can also give a sense of accomplishment and boost a player's self-esteem.

However, excessive gaming, often leading to a behavioral addiction known as Internet Gaming Disorder (IGD), is linked to negative mental health outcomes. This can include increased rates of depression, anxiety, and aggression. The pressure from competitive gameplay and exposure to cyberbullying can also heighten stress levels. In addition, spending too much time gaming can lead to disrupted sleep patterns, which is a major risk factor for mental health issues. For some, gaming becomes an unhealthy form of escapism, allowing them to avoid real-life problems and emotions, which can exacerbate underlying issues.

Happy Fact: 56% of Filipino Gen Z youth says that gaming helps them relieve stress or anxiety.

Sad Fact: In a study of 300 adolescents in Manila, aboiut 18% (53 out of 300) had a high level of online game addiction. Causing many of them to have poor academic performance, social isolation, and mental health issues.

Breaking Down Social Effects:

Excessive gaming can cause teens to become socially isolated, neglecting real-life friendships and interactions. Overreliance on virtual communication weakens their face-to-face social skills. Online gaming also exposes youth to cyberbullying, harassment, and harmful content, which may lead to loneliness and emotional harm. In the Philippines, 21 million gamers are minors.

Levelling Up Social Effects:

Multiplayer online games create a strong sense of community and belonging by promoting teamwork and cooperation, helping youth develop valuable social skills. They also allow teens to build and maintain friendships worldwide. For shy or socially anxious individuals, gaming provides a safe space to connect and grow confidence. In the Philippines, 74% of gamers play multiplayer games, strengthening social connections online.
